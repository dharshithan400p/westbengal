West Bengal, a state in eastern India, has a rich cultural heritage that includes a variety of traditional and folk dances.
These dances reflect the diverse cultural and religious influences of the region, ranging from folk traditions to classical styles

Here are some of the prominent dances of West Bengal:
1. Chhau Dance
Type: Folk/Traditional
Origin: Purulia district of West Bengal (also found in neighboring states like Odisha and Jharkhand).
Description: Chhau is a martial dance that combines elements of folk traditions, martial arts, and storytelling through vibrant masks.
The dance often depicts episodes from epics like the Mahabharata and Ramayana, as well as themes from local folklore.

2. Gambhira Dance
Type: Religious/Folk
Origin: Malda district
Description: A religious folk dance performed during the worship of Lord Shiva.
It involves two characters, usually a grandfather and grandson, engaging in humorous yet philosophical dialogues while addressing societal and religious issues.
